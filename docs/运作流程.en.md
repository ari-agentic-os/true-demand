# 💊 The Willingness-to-Pay Wake-Up Call — Workflow (for growth-stage founders)

> Your MVP already landed its first seed users — but you hit a wall fast: activation is low? You freeze the
> moment charging comes up? Can't tell whether to add features or change direction?
> This skill (internal name `true-demand`) exists to cure the **growth-stage echo-chamber** — it reads the
> truth out of your seed users' *bills*, not their compliments.
>
> **English vibe:** cure your MVP from the **"everyone loves it until it's time to pay"** syndrome.

---

## 📋 Three core commands (中英镜像 · bilingual mirror)

To fit the real research cadence of a growth-stage founder — half a month, sometimes a full month — this
skill drops the "grind it out in one chat window" model. It splits into three hard, **independently
triggerable, locally-persisted** actions. You type the command in whichever language you want; the skill
generates the matching files **in that language** (no Chinese-English salad):

| 中文 | English mirror | What it does | Output (auto-switches by trigger language) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`/开局`** | **`/kickoff`** | Read existing docs + triage shape, pin the biggest *commercial* risk, issue guide + recruiting plan + one behavioral experiment. | `dashboard_hypotheses.md` · `guide_true_demand_interview.md` · `plan_recruit_experiment.md` (zh mirrors `看板_` · `提纲_` · `行动计划_`) |
| **`/纠偏`** | **`/align`** | Play your outside advisor — drop in raw notes, read the subtext, stop you trusting false positives. | interactive in-chat feedback |
| **`/确诊`** | **`/diagnose`** | Synthesize all 5 notes across files, run the opportunity tree + price model, deliver a build/pivot/drop call. | `报告_终局确诊.md` / `report_final_diagnosis.md` |

### 🚀 `/kickoff` — diagnose the now, hand over the guide

* **When:** your MVP has a batch of beta users, but activation is low, no one wants to pay, or the roadmap is a fog.
* **What it does:**
  0. **Read what you already have + triage shape:** if your repo has a PRD / docs / prototype **and any data
     (retention / funnel / tickets / payments — the strongest evidence)**, it scans them
     and extracts the implicit assumptions (as hypotheses to test, not findings), asks only the gaps, and reads
     your **shape** (pre-MVP / growth-stage · B2C / B2B) to bend the method.
  1. **See past the surface risk:** pull your attention off "is the feature usable" and back onto the lens
     founders dodge — **viability** (will they actually pay for this).
  2. **Persist three files locally:** the dashboard `dashboard_hypotheses.md` (with pre-committed **confirm/kill
     lines**) + the Mom Test guide `guide_true_demand_interview.md` + the **action plan**
     `plan_recruit_experiment.md` (how to recruit the right people + one behavioral experiment to run in parallel).
* **⏸️ Default stop — tiny first step:** start with ONE conversation (not "5 then come back") + the experiment,
  and set an implementation intention ("DM 3 people by Wednesday"). Re-entry is cheap — even one note runs `/align`.

### 🎯 `/align` — debrief & translate what users really meant

* **When:** any time during the half-month of user chats when you feel "they're just being polite" or "I'm not sure."
* **What it does:** plays your outside advisor. You drop in raw notes / transcribed audio; it reads the subtext objectively.
  * **Audits the source first:** how were these notes gathered — any pitch, leading questions, only the good bits? Dirty input gets tagged and downweighted before analysis (behavior > stated).
  * *e.g.* shows you that "I'd buy it if my company expensed it" is really *dodging paying out of pocket* —
    the budget owner isn't them; or spots that they happily pay big for something else, opening a new pay-point.
  * Corrects your questioning posture for the next session, so false positives don't fool you.

### 🏁 `/diagnose` — cross-file synthesis & strategic pivot

* **When:** the half-month is up, you've talked to 5 real seed users, and you're sitting on a pile of messy notes.
* **What it does:** the final diagnosis. It reads every record under your local `interview_notes/`, clusters
  real pay-signals and high-value pains with rigorous affinity mapping, runs the Opportunity-Solution Tree
  (Torres), and — **judging each hypothesis against the confirm/kill line you pre-committed at kickoff** — hands
  you a `report_final_diagnosis.md` with a **confidence band** (saturation × behavioral-not-said × cross-segment):
  * **Build:** the core pain is clear and a pay-path works → ship the roadmap.
  * **Pivot:** the current direction is a fake need, but you found a painfully real underlying one — it teaches
    you how to reuse your existing tech assets to pivot elegantly into a scene that earns real money.
  * **Drop:** fully falsified — cut losses, save the time for the next idea.
  * **Loop:** a verdict only closes one *round* — it logs the result on the dashboard, names the next-riskiest assumption, and re-opens `/kickoff` (Build-Measure-Learn); on a Pivot, re-kickoff on the new need.

## 🔬 The methodology matrix (8 hard standards under the plain talk)

The talk goes plain, but the model underneath strictly executes the industry's gold-standard validation bar:

| Dimension | Methodology | The actual gate it enforces in the flow |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Commercial red-line** | **1. IDEO Three Lenses** | `/kickoff` pins 80% of the weight on **viability**, cuts the usability vanity. |
| **Anti-flattery check** | **2. The Mom Test** | Filters out every future-intent pleasantry; surfaces the real *past behavior* in their bills. |
| **Deep-motive mining** | **3. JTBD** | Unpacks the social & emotional pain behind the purchase; diagnoses switching resistance. |
| **Pricing breakthrough** | **4. Van Westendorp PSM** | Via qualitative behavioral comparison, derives a price band that won't churn your beta fans. |
| **Signal convergence** | **5. Thematic saturation / information power (Guest 2006 · Malterud 2016)** | Interview per segment until no new themes (~6 to start, ~12 cap); Nielsen's "5" is usability-only, not for demand/pricing. |
| **De-fog the roadmap** | **6. Teresa Torres Opportunity-Solution Tree** | Organizes messy notes into goal → opportunity → solution → experiment. |
| **Rescue activation** | **7. Superhuman core-path subtraction** | Take one sip from the river — subtract features via superuser feedback to rescue low activation. |
| **Code redemption** | **8. Minimal-tech-asset Pivot** | After falsification, waste not one line of old code — redeploy existing assets into a new, higher-ACV scene. |

## 📂 Local file architecture (English trigger)

When you start with an English command (e.g. `/kickoff`), the skill generates equally crafted, native English
file names instead of Chinese ones — no salad:

```text
projects/true-demand/{Date}_{Project_Name}/
├── dashboard_hypotheses.md        # riskiest assumption · confirm/kill lines · evidence (zh: 看板_核心假设.md)
├── guide_true_demand_interview.md # de-AI'd Mom Test script (zh: 提纲_真需求访谈.md)
├── plan_recruit_experiment.md     # recruiting plan + one behavioral experiment (zh: 行动计划_招募与实验.md)
├── report_final_diagnosis.md      # affinity clusters · build/pivot/drop · confidence (zh: 报告_终局确诊.md)
└── interview_notes/               # (zh: 访谈笔记/) — your raw notes
```

### The constraint on English guide generation

When `/kickoff` writes `guide_true_demand_interview.md`, the underlying Mom Test rules forcibly strip out all
the polished-but-useless "AI-speak English" and replace it with penetrating, native behavior-mining lines:

* ❌ *"What features do you expect in a time-tracking app?"* (invites vanity)
* ❌ *"How much would you pay for this feature?"* (a blank check)
* 🎯 *"How do you currently manage this problem?"*
* 🎯 *"What else have you tried?"*
* 🎯 *"When was the last time you paid for a solution to this, and how much was it?"*

## 🌐 Seamless bilingual switching

The system is **context-driven**. Use Chinese on day one and English the next day to debrief — it reads the
local folder structure and the command language and switches smoothly. No `/set-language` config needed; the
core verb you type *is* the language selector.

### Scenario A — Chinese kickoff

> **用户:** `/开局 我做了一个面向独立创作者的「多平台排版 + 一键同步」浏览器插件，500 个博主留了邮箱拿内测，但大家只用来本地排版、几乎没人点同步...`
> **AI:** （自动识别中文，先读你已有的行为数据，再初始化中文看板 + 访谈提纲 + 行动计划，沉稳靠谱的顾问语气）

### Scenario B — English kickoff

> **User:** `/kickoff I built a multi-platform formatting + one-click cross-posting extension for creators. 500+ bloggers grabbed the beta, but most only use it to beautify posts locally — almost nobody clicks sync. I want to charge $2/mo next month...`
> **AI:**
> 500 creator emails is a classic "effort-cost illusion" — leaving an email is a cheap tool-bet on "writing a
> hit." "Format but don't sync" means you may have built a low-frequency real need (cross-platform
> distribution) as a high-frequency sidekick (a local beautifier).
> You already hold the hardest evidence: most of 500 installs only format, almost nobody syncs — real
> behavioral data, the loudest signal there is.
> I've created three local files: `dashboard_hypotheses.md` (riskiest assumption + pre-committed confirm/kill
> lines), `guide_true_demand_interview.md` (a zero-fluff Mom Test script), and `plan_recruit_experiment.md`
> (who to recruit + a fake-door / pre-sell on "sync" to test real clicks & payment in parallel).
> Step one stays tiny: book ONE of the "weekly-open, never-sync" users first, set an implementation intention
> ("DM 3 by Wednesday"), and even one note re-enters `/align`. `/diagnose` once it saturates. Good luck!
