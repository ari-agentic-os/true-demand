# 💊 The Willingness-to-Pay Wake-Up Call — Workflow (for growth-stage founders)

> Your MVP already landed its first seed users — but you hit a wall fast: activation is low? You freeze the
> moment charging comes up? Can't tell whether to add features or change direction?
> This skill (internal name `true-demand`) exists to cure the **growth-stage echo-chamber** — it reads the
> truth out of your seed users' *bills*, not their compliments.
>
> **English vibe:** cure your MVP from the **"everyone loves it until it's time to pay"** syndrome.

---

## 📋 Three core commands (中英镜像 · bilingual mirror)

To fit the real research cadence of a growth-stage founder — half a month, sometimes a full month — this
skill drops the "grind it out in one chat window" model. It splits into three hard, **independently
triggerable, locally-persisted** actions. You type the command in whichever language you want; the skill
generates the matching files **in that language** (no Chinese-English salad):

| 中文 | English mirror | What it does | Output (auto-switches by trigger language) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`/开局`** | **`/kickoff`** | Diagnose the now, pin the biggest *commercial* risk, issue an anti-flattery interview guide. | `看板_核心假设.md` / `dashboard_hypotheses.md` · `提纲_真需求访谈.md` / `guide_true_demand_interview.md` |
| **`/纠偏`** | **`/align`** | Play your outside advisor — drop in raw notes, pierce the subtext, stop you trusting false positives. | interactive in-chat feedback |
| **`/确诊`** | **`/diagnose`** | Synthesize all 5 notes across files, run the opportunity tree + price model, deliver a build/pivot/drop call. | `报告_终局确诊.md` / `report_final_diagnosis.md` |

### 🚀 `/kickoff` — diagnose the now, issue the weapon

* **When:** your MVP has a batch of beta users, but activation is low, no one wants to pay, or the roadmap is a fog.
* **What it does:**
  1. **Pierce the surface risk:** drag your attention off "is the feature usable" and back onto the lens
     founders dodge — **viability** (will they actually pay for this).
  2. **Persist context locally:** auto-creates your validation dashboard at
     `projects/true-demand/{Date}_{Project_Name}/dashboard_hypotheses.md`.
  3. **Issue a plain-spoken guide:** outputs a hand-de-AI'd (via `tool-humanizer` if installed, else
     self-edited) Mom Test script you can take straight to beta users — `guide_true_demand_interview.md`.
* **⏸️ Default stop:** the AI steps off here and hands you the stage — go talk to 5 real users with the guide.

### 🎯 `/align` — debrief & translate what users really meant

* **When:** any time during the half-month of user chats when you feel "they're just being polite" or "I'm not sure."
* **What it does:** plays your outside advisor. You drop in raw notes / transcribed audio; it reads the subtext objectively.
  * *e.g.* shows you that "I'd buy it if my company expensed it" is really *dodging paying out of pocket* —
    the budget owner isn't them; or spots that they happily pay big for something else, opening a new pay-point.
  * Corrects your questioning posture for the next session, so false positives don't fool you.

### 🏁 `/diagnose` — cross-file synthesis & strategic pivot

* **When:** the half-month is up, you've talked to 5 real seed users, and you're sitting on a pile of messy notes.
* **What it does:** the final diagnosis. It reads every record under your local `interview_notes/`, clusters
  real pay-signals and high-value pains with rigorous affinity mapping, runs the Opportunity-Solution Tree
  (Torres), and hands you a clean `report_final_diagnosis.md`:
  * **Build:** the core pain is clear and a pay-path works → ship the roadmap.
  * **Pivot:** the current direction is a fake need, but you found a bleeding-real underlying one — it teaches
    you how to reuse your existing tech assets to pivot elegantly into a scene that earns real money.
  * **Drop:** fully falsified — cut losses, save the time for the next idea.

## 🔬 The methodology matrix (8 hard standards under the plain talk)

The talk goes plain, but the model underneath strictly executes the industry's gold-standard validation bar:

| Dimension | Methodology | The actual gate it enforces in the flow |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Commercial red-line** | **1. IDEO Three Lenses** | `/kickoff` pins 80% of the weight on **viability**, cuts the usability vanity. |
| **Anti-flattery lie test** | **2. The Mom Test** | Filters out every future-intent pleasantry; forces out the real *past behavior* in their bills. |
| **Deep-motive mining** | **3. JTBD** | Unpacks the social & emotional pain behind the purchase; diagnoses switching resistance. |
| **Pricing breakthrough** | **4. Van Westendorp PSM** | Via qualitative behavioral comparison, derives a price band that won't churn your beta fans. |
| **Signal convergence** | **5. Nielsen's 5-user law** | Locks exactly 5 deep qualitative interviews → ~85% signal saturation → ends endless research. |
| **De-fog the roadmap** | **6. Teresa Torres Opportunity-Solution Tree** | Organizes messy notes into goal → opportunity → solution → experiment. |
| **Rescue activation** | **7. Superhuman core-path subtraction** | Take one sip from the river — subtract features via superuser feedback to rescue low activation. |
| **Code redemption** | **8. Minimal-tech-asset Pivot** | After falsification, waste not one line of old code — squeeze existing assets into a new, higher-ACV scene. |

## 📂 Local file architecture (English trigger)

When you start with an English command (e.g. `/kickoff`), the skill generates equally crafted, native English
file names instead of Chinese ones — no salad:

```text
projects/true-demand/{Date}_{Project_Name}/
├── dashboard_hypotheses.md        # (zh: 看板_核心假设.md)
├── guide_true_demand_interview.md # (zh: 提纲_真需求访谈.md)
├── report_final_diagnosis.md      # (zh: 报告_终局确诊.md)
└── interview_notes/               # (zh: 访谈笔记/) — your 5 raw English notes
```

### The constraint on English guide generation

When `/kickoff` writes `guide_true_demand_interview.md`, the underlying Mom Test rules forcibly strip out all
the polished-but-useless "AI-speak English" and replace it with penetrating, native behavior-mining lines:

* ❌ *"What features do you expect in a time-tracking app?"* (invites vanity)
* ❌ *"How much would you pay for this feature?"* (a blank check)
* 🎯 *"How do you currently manage this problem?"*
* 🎯 *"What else have you tried?"*
* 🎯 *"When was the last time you paid for a solution to this, and how much was it?"*
