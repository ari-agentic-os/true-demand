# 07 · Recruiting (the real bottleneck — get the right 5 in the room)

Issuing the guide is ~10% of the work; getting 5 of the **right** users to actually talk is the 90% that
stalls founders. Kickoff must hand over a recruiting plan, not just questions.

## Who to talk to (the right 5, not any 5)

- **Best source = your own seed list / the channel that already converted.** They hold the viability truth.
- **Prioritize:** engaged-but-not-converting · recently churned · power users. These three expose why money
  does or doesn't move.
- **Avoid:** friends/family, random sign-ups, "anyone willing" — they bias toward 客套 (politeness).
- **One segment at a time.** Don't blend B2C consumers with B2B buyers; recruit and read them separately.

## Where to find them (go where they already are)

- **Existing:** waitlist, beta list, support tickets, the one channel that worked (e.g. 小红书 / 即刻 / Discord / a subreddit).
- **Warm:** intros from current users — "谁还在为这事头疼？" / "who else struggles with this?"
- **Cold (last resort):** communities where the job is discussed; reviewers of competitors.

## Outreach (ask for help, not a sales call)

Short, specific, low-pressure, no leading. Adapt to the working language:

- **Cold:** "嗨 {name}，看到你在 {场景} 折腾 {问题}。我在研究这块，不卖东西，想请教你 15 分钟现在是怎么搞的，这周方便吗？" /
  "Hi {name} — saw you wrestling with {problem}. I'm researching this (not selling). Could I borrow 15 min on how you handle it today?"
- **To your own users:** "你是少数每周都打开的人，想请教你的工作流，15 分钟，帮我把产品做对。" /
  "You're one of the few who opens it weekly — can I learn your workflow for 15 min?"

## Incentives

Often unneeded when you ask for help on *their* problem. If needed: a small gift card, a free month, or
sharing the findings back. Don't over-pay strangers — it attracts professional respondents who tell you what
you want to hear.

## Logistics & no-shows

- 15–20 min; offer async voice notes if scheduling is hard.
- **Over-recruit ~2×** (line up 10 to land 5). Confirm day-of. Offer two slots.
- **Record with consent** — so `/align` reads the real thing, not biased after-the-fact notes.

## Adapt by shape (see `09-situation-triage.md`)

- **Pre-MVP (no users of your own):** you can't mine a seed list — recruit from communities where the problem
  is discussed, competitors' users/reviewers, and warm intros. Ask about the *problem and past spend*, never
  about your not-yet-built product.
- **B2B / enterprise:** fewer, heavier interviews, and you must pass gatekeepers. Talk to all three roles —
  the **end-user**, the **champion**, and the **budget owner** — separately; their answers differ. "公司报销"
  is not self-pay willingness. One signed LOI or paid pilot outweighs ten enthusiastic end-users.

## Output (write into the action plan)

A recruiting plan: the target segment · the exact source list · a ready-to-send outreach message · the
over-recruit target · incentive (if any). Goes in `行动计划_招募与实验.md` / `plan_recruit_experiment.md`.
