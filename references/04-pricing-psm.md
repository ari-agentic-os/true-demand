# 04 · Pricing & Willingness-to-Pay (no-churn band for a beta base)

Pricing is a *viability* question — the one founders most often guess, and the exact spot where "一收费全员
卸载" happens. The job: find a price that converts without churning the beta fans you already have. Behavior
beats stated intent, always.

## First rule: behavior beats "would you pay?"

The strongest WTP signal is what they **already pay** for this job today (from the interview, ref 03 §3).
A real ¥/$/mo on an existing tool tells you more than any "I'd pay $X." Everyone says yes to a hypothetical.

## Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter

> Source: Peter van Westendorp (1976). Ask AFTER they understand the value:
1. So **expensive** you wouldn't consider it? (too expensive)
2. **Expensive but worth considering**? (high)
3. A **bargain**? (good value)
4. So **cheap** you'd doubt the quality? (too cheap)

With ~5–20 interviews this is **directional, not statistical** — a conversation scaffold, not a number to
bank. The acceptable band sits between the "too cheap" and "too expensive" crossovers.

## The no-churn read (growth-stage specific)

You have seed users who got it free. Two prices matter:
- **The new-user price** (what a cold buyer would pay) — from PSM + past spend.
- **The grandfather/anchor** — what your beta fans tolerate. Charging often churns the freeloaders who were
  never going to pay anyway; that's *signal*, not loss. Watch who churns: if your *engaged* users leave at
  any price, the value is the problem, not the price.

## Lighter probes for small-n

- **Anchor on alternatives:** "你现在为 {替代方案} 花多少？" / "What do you pay for {current workaround}?"
- **The give-up test:** "有没有买过类似的然后停了？为什么？" / "Ever paid for something like this and stopped — why?"
- **Budget owner:** "这钱从谁的预算出？" / "Whose budget?" (no owner = weak viability)

## Sell-before-you-build (strongest signal)

A pre-order, paid pilot, "上线发我，这是我卡" / "email me when it's live, here's my card." Commitment of money/
time/reputation outranks any survey — log it as the highest-confidence viability evidence. See
`08-behavioral-experiments.md` for the full experiment menu and the evidence ladder.

## B2B / enterprise (who actually pays)

The user, the champion, and the budget owner are three different people. Price to the **budget owner's ROI**,
not the end-user's delight, and confirm the approval/procurement path. "公司会报销" / "the company will expense
it" is *not* willingness to pay — find out whose budget it is and whether it's approved. Strongest signal = a
signed LOI or paid pilot (`08`).

## Output
An acceptable price band (or "no willingness found"), who pays, on what model (subscription / one-time /
take-rate / freemium), and a confidence level (behavioral > stated). Feed into `02-diagnose.md`.
