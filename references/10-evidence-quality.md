# 10 · Evidence quality (inputs are guilty until clean)

`/align` and `/diagnose` are only as good as the notes the founder feeds them. Founders lead witnesses, record
selectively, and hear "支持" as "愿意付费." Three guards before you trust a single note — this is the *quality*
gate that feeds the *quantity* gate (`03` §B saturation) and the *decision* gate (`02` rubric).

## 1. Capture: separate the rungs (Ladder of Inference)

A note has four fields — never blend them:

| Field | What goes here |
|---|---|
| **DID** | behavior / past spend — with numbers & dates ("paid ¥39/mo for Notion since Jan") |
| **SAID** | exact words, verbatim, in quotes |
| **COMMITTED** | real currency — money / time / reputation / a referral (Mom Test "commitment currency") |
| **MY READ** | your interpretation — clearly flagged, kept apart from the three above |

Prefer a **recording / transcript** over memory (consent first) — it's the only way `/align` sees the real
interview, not the founder's hopeful reconstruction.

## 2. Audit the interviewer FIRST (before you analyze)

Check *how* the notes were gathered before trusting them:

- **Did you pitch?** A pitch contaminates everything said after it.
- **Leading vs behavioral:** "would you pay?" (leading) vs "when did you last pay, and how much?" (behavioral).
- **Selective recording:** only the enthusiastic bits? Was any *disconfirming* question asked?
- **Commitment or praise?** A real commitment, or just polite encouragement?

Tag each note **clean / leading / pitch-contaminated** and downweight accordingly.

## 3. Did > Said (downweight stated-only)

Behavioral evidence outranks attitudinal — carry the verdicts from `/align` into `/diagnose`. A theme that is
all SAID, no DID, is a **hypothesis, not evidence**, and cannot support a Build call (evidence ladder, `08`).

> of Inference; social-desirability bias (Edwards 1957) & demand characteristics (Orne 1962); Lincoln & Guba
