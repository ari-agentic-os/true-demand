# 09 · Situation triage (read the founder's shape before you advise)

Run this right after the context scan (Step 0) and before the gated discovery. **Don't make the founder fill a
form** — infer both axes from the scan + their opening, state your read in one line, let them correct it, and
ask a real question only if it's genuinely ambiguous. The default audience is growth-stage B2C; the method
must bend for the other shapes or it hands out ill-fitting advice.

## Axis 1 — Stage

| Stage | Tell | Riskiest default | What bends |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-MVP** (idea/prototype, ~no users) | no real users, no retention/revenue | does this problem even exist, and is it painful enough? (desirability **and** viability) | you can't mine your own users (none) → recruit from communities / competitors' users / warm intros; experiment = smoke test / landing / concierge; ask about the *problem & past spend*, never your not-yet-built product |
| **Growth-stage** (seed users, stalled) — *default* | has users but low activation / can't charge / roadmap fog | will they actually pay? (pure viability) | the standard flow: pre-sell / payment link / fake-door; talk to engaged-but-not-converting & churned |
| **Post-PMF / scaling** | real, growing revenue | *(out of scope)* | say so plainly: this skill *finds* demand, it doesn't scale it — redirect |

## Axis 2 — Who buys

| Type | Tell | Who pays | What bends |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B2C / prosumer** — *default* | individual user, consumer pricing | the user is the payer | standard recruiting (communities, own list); thresholds per `08` |
| **B2B / enterprise** | "for teams", "company will expense it", procurement, seats | **champion ≠ end-user ≠ budget owner** — three different people | recruit through gatekeepers (fewer, heavier interviews); talk to all three roles separately; "公司报销" ≠ self-pay willingness; experiment = LOI / paid pilot / a real budget-owner conversation; price to ROI, not delight; lower n, each counts more |

## How to use it

1. **Infer** both axes from Step 0 + the opening paragraph.
2. **State the read in one line**, invite correction: 「看起来你是 {成长期} 的 {B2C}，对吗？」 / "Looks like you're {growth-stage} {B2C} — right?"
3. **Branch** the rest of kickoff: the riskiest-assumption default, the who-pays model, recruiting (`07`), the experiment (`08`), and the pricing read (`04`).
4. **Ask only if truly ambiguous** (e.g. solo consumer app vs small-team tool).

Keep it to that one confirm line — triage is a *read*, not an interrogation.
